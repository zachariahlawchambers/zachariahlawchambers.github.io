Refugee Law

If you've made a refugee claim in Canada, or you're thinking about making one, I can help you understand your options and what to expect.

What I help with

  • Refugee claims before the Refugee Protection Division (RPD)
  • Claims based on sexual orientation, gender identity, and gender expression (SOGIE)
  • Refugee appeals before the Refugee Appeal Division (RAD)
  • Pre-removal risk assessments (PRRA)
  • Humanitarian and compassionate applications related to refugee matters
  • Related immigration matters that arise from a refugee claim

If this is your situation

If you've recently arrived in Canada and need to make a refugee claim, there are deadlines that apply quickly. Get legal help as soon as you can.

If your claim has already been heard and refused, you may have the right to appeal. Time limits apply, so don't wait.

If your hearing is coming up and you don't have a lawyer yet, contact me. I'll let you know whether I can help and what the next steps would be.

My experience

I have particular experience with refugee claims from Ethiopia, India and Africa. I've worked with clients who speak many different languages and come from many different backgrounds.

I understand that this is a very stressful time. I'll explain the process clearly and let you know what to expect at each stage.

Legal Aid Ontario

I take Legal Aid Ontario certificates for immigration and refugee matters. If you have one or think you might qualify, get in touch.

You can check whether you qualify at legalaid.on.ca.

Useful resources

Some organizations that can help with information and support:

  • Immigration and Refugee Board of Canada (IRB): the tribunal that hears refugee claims
  • Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)
  • Legal Aid Ontario: find out if you qualify for a certificate
  • CLEO (Community Legal Education Ontario): plain-language guides to refugee and immigration law
